## Overview

### Academic Year & Semester Structure
The TA employment process runs on a **per-semester cycle**. One academic year consists of three semesters:

| Semester | Label Pattern | Example |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| Semester 1 | `[YY]-1` | `69-1` |
| Semester 2 | `[YY]-2` | `69-2` |
| Summer | `[YY]-summer` | `69-summer` |

- The year component uses the **Thai Buddhist Era (B.E.)** year (e.g., `69` = B.E. 2569).
- Staff refer to semesters using the shorthand pattern **`b.e. year-semester`** (e.g., `69-1`, `69-2`, `69-summer`).
- Each semester triggers a new iteration of the full TA employment process loop.

### Process Overview (5 Main Steps)
The employment process follows five sequential steps each semester:

| Step | Thai Name | English Translation |
|:----:|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | วางแผนงบประมาณ | Budget Planning |
| 2 | สอบถามยอดผู้ช่วยสอน | TA Quota Inquiry |
| 3 | รับสมัครผู้ช่วยสอน | TA Recruitment & Application |
| 4 | แต่งตั้งผู้ช่วยสอน | TA Appointment |
| 5 | เบิกจ่าย | Disbursement & Payment |

> Detailed procedures for each step are covered in the dedicated sections below.

## TA Types & Classifications

There are seven TA types, each with a designated hourly payment rate:

| # | TA Type | Program | Hourly Rate (THB) |
|---|---------|---------|:-----------------:|
| 1 | Graduate Assistant | Thai Program | 200 |
| 2 | Graduate Assistant | International Program | 300 |
| 3 | Bachelor Student Assistant | Thai Program | 90 |
| 4 | Bachelor Student Assistant | International Program | 120 |
| 5 | Master Student Assistant | International Program | 300 |
| 6 | Lab Head | — | 450 |
| 7 | CAMT Staff | — | 0 (no payment) |

> **Note:** CAMT Staff TAs receive no monetary compensation (0 THB/hour), as their TA duties fall within their staff role.

### Special Case: Master Student Assistant (International Program)
Although listed as a distinct TA type, the **Master Student Assistant (International Program)** is handled differently from the others:

- **In EMS:** Recorded as **Graduate Assistant (International Program)** — the system does not have a separate option for Master Student Assistants, and there is currently no plan to add one.
- **Payment rate:** Same as Graduate Assistant (International Program) — **300 THB/hour**.
- **Appointment process:** A new university rule (effective from a recent past semester) requires Master Student Assistants to be **แต่งตั้ง (appointed) through a separate process**, unlike Graduate Assistants who follow the standard TA employment flow. See Step 4 for details.

## Step 1 — Budget Planning (วางแผนงบประมาณ)

### Frequency
This step occurs **once per academic year** (not per semester).

### Timing
- Typically worked on in **August–September**.
- The completed budget plan must be submitted to Finance Staff **before October**, in alignment with the **Thai government fiscal year** deadline.

### Coverage
One budget plan covers three semesters spanning two academic years:
- Semester 2 of the **current** academic year
- Summer of the **current** academic year
- Semester 1 of the **next** academic year

### Objective
Estimate the total expected TA employment costs for the covered semesters, broken down by major, course, section, and position.

### Input Data Sources
Two main data sources are used:

| Source | Description | Provided by |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Historical data | TA assignment records from the past 3–4 academic years | TA Admin Staff |
| Timetable files | Draft course-opening plan for the upcoming semesters (Excel files) | Academic Service Staff (เจ้าหน้าที่สาขา) of each major |

#### Timetable File Format & Ideal Content
The timetable files are Excel (.xlsx) files, one per major per semester. Each file:
- Is named in the pattern: `(ร่าง) ตารางสอน [MAJOR] [YY]-[SEMESTER].xlsx` (e.g., `(ร่าง) ตารางสอน ANI 69-1.xlsx`)
- Contains **multiple sheets**, typically one per student year group (e.g., ปี1, ปี2, ปี3, ปี4) plus shared/GE course sheets
- Uses a **visual grid layout**: days of the week as rows, time slots (hourly) as columns
- Each occupied cell shows: course code / section number, instructor name, student count, and room
- Includes a **course summary table** at the bottom of each sheet listing course code, course name, and credit hours

> **Ideal (not yet consistent):** TA Admin Staff encourages เจ้าหน้าที่สาขา to include **ยอดผู้ช่วยสอน (TA quota data)** — i.e., which sections need a TA and how many — directly in these timetable files. In practice, this has not been done consistently, and the reminder is issued every semester. When the data is included, it reduces the manual estimation work in Step 2.

### Process
1. Collect timetable files from each major's Academic Service Staff (เจ้าหน้าที่สาขา).
2. Review historical TA assignment data from the past 3–4 academic years.
3. List expected opening courses at the **section level** for each covered semester.
4. For each section, determine expected TA **positions** (type, hours, weeks).
5. Calculate expected cost per position: `weeks × hours_per_week × hourly_rate`.
6. Aggregate costs by section → course → major → grand total.

### Outputs
| Output | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Expected open positions | Per section, per course — with TA type, hours, and weeks |
| Expected cost per position | Calculated from class pattern and TA type rate |
| Total expected cost per major | Sum of all positions within each major |
| Grand total | Sum of all majors' expected costs |

### Responsible Parties
- **TA Admin Staff** — leads the planning and produces the budget plan
- **Academic Service Staff (เจ้าหน้าที่สาขา)** — provides timetable files
- **Finance Staff** — receives and processes the submitted budget plan

## Step 2 — TA Quota Inquiry (สอบถามยอดผู้ช่วยสอน)

### Frequency & Timing
This step occurs **once per semester**, typically **~2 months before the semester starts**.

### Objective
Confirm which courses actually need a TA and how many positions are required, based on input from instructors.

### System: EMS (Education Management System)
All data entry and instructor interaction for this step is done through the CAMT Education Management System.
- **URL:** `ems-admin.camt.cmu.ac.th`
- **Recommended:** Use on a desktop/laptop. If on mobile, enable "Request desktop site."

### Process

**Phase 1 — Data Input by TA Admin Staff**

1. Take the expected open positions from the Step 1 budget plan.
2. Enter each position as a row in the EMS system under the current semester.

> **Note:** Ideally, เจ้าหน้าที่สาขา would include ยอดผู้ช่วยสอน data in the timetable files (see Step 1), which would make this data input more accurate and reduce estimation. In practice this has not been done consistently; TA Admin Staff reminds them each semester.

**Position table structure (one row = one TA position):**

| Column (Thai) | Column (English) | Description |
|---|---|---|
| รหัสวิชา | Course Code | Subject code |
| ตอนที่ | Section No. | Section number (see note on multi-TA sections below) |
| หลักสูตร | Program | Major code (e.g., ANI, DF) |
| ผู้สอน | Instructor | Primary instructor's name |
| ประเภทการสอน | Class Type | LEC (Lecture) or LAB |
| วัน | Day(s) | Teaching day(s) of the week |
| เวลาเริ่ม | Start Time | Class start time |
| ถึงเวลา | End Time | Class end time |
| โปรดเลือกประเภทผู้ช่วยสอน | TA Type (to select) | Dropdown for instructor to choose TA type |
| ผู้ช่วยสอนที่เลือก | Selected TA | Filled after TA is selected in a later step |
| ค่าจ้างสูงสุด | Maximum Wage | Auto-calculated based on TA type and class pattern |
| ค่าจ้างจริง | Actual Wage | Filled after the semester (actual hours worked) |
| เกินที่คาดการณ์ | Exceeds Estimate | Flags if actual wage exceeds the budget plan estimate |

**Multi-TA sections:** Normally, one section = one TA position (one row). When a section requires two TAs, two rows are created and the section number in "ตอนที่" is appended with an order number:
- First position: `001-1`
- Second position: `001-2`

---

**Phase 2 — TA Type Selection by Instructors**

Once TA Admin Staff completes data entry, the system is opened for instructors to review their sections and select the TA type for each position.

Instructors have two actions available:

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Section already exists in the table | Click the **"Select an option"** dropdown in the "โปรดเลือกประเภทผู้ช่วยสอน" column to choose a TA type |
| Section is missing from the table | Click **"สร้างการขอใช้ผู้ช่วยสอน"** to create a new section entry, then fill in the full form |

---

### Full Position Form Fields
Each position entry (whether pre-loaded or created by the instructor) contains the following fields:

| Field (Thai) | Field (English) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ภาคเรียน | Semester | e.g., `2569. ภาคการศึกษาที่ 1` |
| รหัสวิชา | Course Code | e.g., `951106` |
| ชื่อวิชา | Course Name | e.g., `Screenwriting and Digital Storytelling` |
| ตอนที่ | Section No. | e.g., `001` |
| หลักสูตร | Program | e.g., `ANI` |
| ผู้สอน | Primary Instructor | Main instructor for the section |
| ผู้สอนอื่นๆ | Other Instructors | Additional instructors who can also access and edit this TA request |
| ประเภทการสอน | Class Type | Lecture / LAB |
| วันที่สอน | Teaching Day(s) | One or more days, e.g., `Tue`, `Fri` |
| เวลาเริ่ม | Start Time | e.g., `11:00:00` |
| เวลาสิ้นสุด | End Time | e.g., `12:30:00` |
| ประเภทผู้ช่วยสอน | TA Type | Selected from the TA type list (e.g., `จบ ป.ตรี (TH)`) |
| โน้ต | Note | Free-text note field; instructors typically use this to suggest a specific TA candidate by name, or to state a specific requirement for the position |

### EMS TA Type Dropdown — Important Note
The TA type dropdown in EMS does **not** include "Master Student Assistant (International Program)" as a separate option. Master Student Assistants are recorded in the system as **Graduate Assistant (International Program)**.

**Background:** Master Student Assistants were previously employed under the Graduate Assistant category. A new university rule (effective from a recent past semester) now requires Master Student Assistants to be แต่งตั้ง (appointed) through a **separate process**. However:
- The payment rate remains the same (300 THB/hour) for both types.
- The EMS system has not been updated to reflect this distinction, and there is currently no plan to update it.

> ⚠️ When a position in EMS shows "Graduate Assistant (International Program)," it may represent either a Graduate Assistant or a Master Student Assistant in practice. The actual appointment process for Master Student Assistants follows a different path (see Step 4).

### Responsible Parties
- **TA Admin Staff** — inputs initial data from the budget plan into EMS; opens system for instructors
- **Instructor** — selects TA type for each position; may add missing sections

### Phase 2 — Instructor Review of Applications

Approximately **1 week after the recruitment announcement**, TA Admin Staff notifies instructors to log in to EMS and review the applications for their sections.

Each instructor can take one of three actions on each application:

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Accept** | Approve the applicant as the TA for that position |
| **Reject** | Decline the applicant |
| **Move to another section** | Reassign the applicant's application to a different section |

### Responsible Parties
- **TA Admin Staff** — prepares and posts the announcement on Facebook; manages the EMS recruitment portal; notifies instructors to review applications after ~1 week
- **Instructor** — reviews applicants and responds with accept, reject, or move to another section

## Duties & Responsibilities

### Class Patterns
TAs are assigned to assist classes. Two class pattern types exist:

**Regular Class** — follows one of the standard weekly schedules:

| Pattern | Days/Week | Hours/Day | Total Hours/Week |
|---------|:---------:|:---------:|:----------------:|
| A | 2 | 1.5 | 3.0 |
| B | 1 | 4.0 | 4.0 |
| C | 1 | 2.0 | 2.0 |
| D | 1 | 3.0 | 3.0 |

**Module Class** — uses the same Regular Class patterns in the system (e.g., 1 day/week, 3 hours/day), but the actual scheduling is determined by the instructor and may differ from a standard weekly cadence. For example, a class assigned the "1 day/week, 3 hours/day" pattern might instead be scheduled on consecutive weekdays for 3 weeks in a row.

### Rules & Constraints
- A TA may assist a class for a **maximum of 15 weeks per semester**.
- A TA **may assist multiple classes** within the same semester, provided there are no schedule conflicts between assigned classes.

## Step 5 — Disbursement & Payment (เบิกจ่าย)

### Hourly Rates
See [TA Types & Classifications](#ta-types--classifications) for the full rate table.

### Payment Calculation
A TA's payment for a given class assignment is based on actual weeks assisted and the class's hourly pattern:

```
Payment = weeks_assisted × hours_per_week × hourly_rate
```

- **`weeks_assisted`** — the number of weeks the TA actually assisted the class (not necessarily the full semester).
- **`hours_per_week`** — determined by the class pattern (e.g., 3 hrs/week for the 1 day × 3 hrs pattern).
- **`hourly_rate`** — determined by the TA's type (see TA Types & Classifications).

**Maximum vs. actual payment:**
- The **maximum possible payment** = 15 weeks × hours_per_week × hourly_rate (i.e., assisting every eligible week of the semester).
- The **actual payment may be less** if the TA did not assist in some weeks, or if no class was held in certain weeks (e.g., public holidays, cancelled sessions).

**Multiple class assignments:**
- If a TA assists more than one class, payment is calculated per class and summed.
